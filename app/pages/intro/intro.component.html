Overview

Get the lowdown on the key pieces of Goose's infrastructure, including our approach to better, faster, stronger web development.

HTML5 doctype

Goose makes use of certain HTML elements and CSS properties that require the use of the HTML5 doctype. Include it at the beginning of all your projects.

Goose Frame

Using the goose frame will not affect any third party plug-ins, as it is fully encapsulated.

To implement this all you would need to is add the g-html and g-body tags.

Disable Zooming

You can disable zooming capabilities on mobile devices by adding user-scalable=no to the viewport meta tag. This disables zooming, meaning users are only able to scroll, and results in your site feeling a bit more like a native application. Overall, we don't recommend this on every site, so use caution!

Containers

Goose requires a containing element to wrap project contents and house our grid system. You may choose one of two containers to use in your projects. Note that, due to padding and more, neither container is nestable.
